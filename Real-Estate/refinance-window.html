When Should I Sell or Refinance?

This series previously found ∑n/p, the rate of equity buildup in a mortgage and Gn, the performance of a real estate investment after n months, in terms of the mortgage months-to-value ratio (RN), inflation (π) and an opportunity cost (ω) of other investments.

Gn = LRN ( cω ( ωn-1 ω-1 ) - ωπ ( (ωπ) n -1 (ωπ) -1 ) + ∑np -cT ) for ω>π>1

where c=qp is a bargain factor, T= F1 +F2 q is time underwater and L= p×RN E0 +F1 is the effective leverage ratio.

For example if G=0.1, this would be like saying your IRA is up 10% over n months. Like other lowercase/capital variables, we define g=1+G as the fractional performance of a real estate investment over n months. From this, the average fractional yield (y) is a geometric average, i.e. the nth root of g.

y= g 1/n = gh

When bought with debt, the yield of real estate investments Y=y-1 begins to depend on n, the number of months a mortgage is held before selling or refinancing. To optimize we take the derivative of the fractional yield using partial derivatives and the chain rule.

dy dn = ∂y ∂g × dg dn + ∂y ∂h × dh dn
dy dn = h gh-1 × dg dn + ln(g) g1n × -1 n2
dy dn = g1/n n ( 1g dg dn - lngn )

Setting the derivative to zero and substituting for G gives our optimization equation for n, for n>0.

n dG dn = (G+1) ln (G+1)

We define Ω=ω-1, Π=π-1 and R=ω/π-1 and make three assumptions to simplify real estate performance G over n months:

  1. Monthly opportunity cost yield is positive but significantly less than one and monthly inflation is positive but less than private opportunity cost.
    0<Ω≪1
    0<Π<Ω ⇒ 0<R<Ω
  2. Based on assumption 1, first-order Maclaurin Series approximations can be made for their exponential functions. Using a standard (X=x-1) upper/lowercase fractional/differential form:
    xn≈ ln(n)

To help determine an approximate time for peak yields, there are three assumptions we will use.

  1. Inflation and interest rates are nearly equal.
  2. Inflation is low enough a first order approximation of the natural log holds.
  3. The leverage ratio is sufficiently large and external yield (X) sufficiently flat that the peak net yield (Y) occurs nearly concurrently with equity yield (y).
