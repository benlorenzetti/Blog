Leverage Ratio

Aug. 2021

The marginal dividend model calculates a target principal size for home loans from rent, expenses, and mortgage rates based on a break even point where monthly income equals expenses for rental properties. Then, expected profits are due to how the purchase price and down payment compare to this target principal size.

The leverage ratio (L) is the ratio of loan size to down payment. From previous articles in this series, the loan size (property price P minus down payment E0) is also equal to the monthly mortgage payment (p) times a constant (Rm) set by interest rates.

The net monthly yield of a property is due to net income from rent after expenses and mortgage payments, and due to equity buildup (ΔEn) over a period of n months from mortgage payments and appreciation.

Both of these portions depend strongly on the leverage ratio. In the past, the leverage ratio has been fairly constrained by federal reserve requirements, federal law, and quasi-public/private entities. However it is still important to know the effect of the leverage ratio when settling on a down payment. The optimum leverage ratio (L0) can be calculated for all reasonable cases where the sales price exceeds the target principal size (P > inc*Rm).
