Of Lexis and Discord

Jan. 10, 2018

Close your weary eyes child for you have seen
The muzzled casting off of Neutrality,
Shorn seems of society, split encodings of reality.

Rest now but raise a wary eye tonight
For as the sun, memory of her fight will want to fade,
but when she appears again with the weather Gage,—

Bear the marathon torch aloft from belfry‐arch,
Through concrete jungles and fiber webs, as a coded signal in flight,—
One if by land, and three if by sea,

Or null lest your light shine across state's boundary;
And two, your salute reserved for the redcoat army,—
Nay sons of liberty, let two be reserved for signaling victory, raised digits heralding a new American bit parity.

Views expressed are those of Mr. Lorenzetti, not the Department of Defense.
